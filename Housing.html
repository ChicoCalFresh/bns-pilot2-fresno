Housing

The following questions were asked in regard to students’ current and past housing situations. This includes changes due to COVID, if they are living near their college (with most schools being remote at the time of the survey), the type of home they currently live in, and hardships that they faced in the past year with housing.

COVID Housing Impact

Has your housing situation changed due to COVID-19? (n=122, 91% of 134 reporting).




Figure 4.1: Impact of COVID on Housing

There are 93 people that participated in the survey that claim their housing has not changed due to the impacts of COVID. There are also 29 people that say their housing situation did change due to the impacts of COVID.


Living Near College

Are you currently living in the city/area in which you are enrolled in college? (n=126, 94% of 134 reporting).



Figure 4.2: Living Near Enrolled College

There are 50 people that participated in the survey that do not currently live in the city/area of their enrolled college. There are also 76 people that say they do live in the city/area of their enrolled college.

Unstable Housing

In the past 12 months, if you have experienced unstable housing, what was the most common reason? (n=25, 19% of 134 reporting). Figure 4.4: Unstable Housing Identification

The data shows that of the people who experienced unstable housing, the most common cause was not having enough money to cover housing expenses (13 people). The next most common cause was from issues with roommates, both having conflicts (4 people) and them being unable/unwilling to pay living expenses (2 people).


Housing Insecure & Homeless

  • Students with at least 3 housing insecurity indicators present are housing insecure, while those with 2 or fewer are not considered housing insecure (n=110, 82% of 134 reporting).
  • Students with at least 1 homelessness insecurity indicator present are considered to have experienced homelessness at the date of survey (n=123, 92% of 134 reporting).

  • The data indicates that the majority of students did not experience homelessness in either the past month (120 students, 97.6%) or year (114 students, 92.7%).
